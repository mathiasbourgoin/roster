## 0. What this document is, and what "anchored" means

The roster is a multi-agent coding harness: a pipeline of skills/agents that move a task through intake → question → research → spec → plan → implement → review → qa → ship, plus standalone auditors (audit / investigate / init). Many of its load-bearing gates today are **agent-asserted** — a model reads prose and self-reports "GO", "VALIDATED", "size is fine", "covered", "high confidence". This is the "doigt mouillé" (wet-finger-in-the-wind) problem: the same input can yield different verdicts across runs, and a green checkmark can be written without the underlying gate having actually passed (Goodhart's law: when a proxy becomes a target, it stops measuring what it proxied).

The fix is to replace *self-judgment* with a **sound external verifier** wherever one exists, and to keep human/agent judgment only where no sound verifier can exist.

Every proposal below carries an **anchor**, one or both of:
- **(INTERNAL)** a specific roster skill spot the audit quoted (e.g. `roster-review.md` Step 6 GO/NO-GO), establishing the behavior exists today;
- **(RESEARCH)** a referenced study/tool from the research doc, with its **STRENGTH label** (STRONG / MODERATE / WEAK-CONTESTED), establishing whether the evidence supports treating the check as a *correctness gate* or only as an *advisory signal*.

A proposal with neither anchor is dropped or marked **"unanchored — opinion only."**

**Key definitions.**
- *Sound external verifier* — a checker whose verdict does not depend on the generating model's introspection (a test exit code, a SARIF `level: error`, a policy engine's exit). Research Theme A (STRONG).
- *Manufactured oracle* — where no reference answer exists, a checker derived from a stated invariant: metamorphic relation, property-based test (PBT), or differential comparison. Research Theme C (STRONG).
- *Firm gate* — a hard pass/fail that can block the human gate or a merge.
- *Advisory signal* — a measured number surfaced for human triage that **must not** hard-block, because the evidence does not support it as a correctness oracle.
- *Keep-agentic* — a judgment that must not be mechanized at all; mechanizing it would manufacture false confidence.

**The single most important reframing in this document** (Requirement 2): the audit's code-metric-threshold gates — P3/P7/P19, i.e. cyclomatic complexity, function/file length, and duplication ratio measured against fixed thresholds like `max_function_lines: 50` or `0.15` duplication — are **DOWNGRADED from firm correctness gates to advisory, locally-calibrated signals.** The research (Theme J, WEAK-CONTESTED) shows the *measurement* is deterministic but the *threshold* is folklore for defect prediction. The proof-of-execution that *captures* those numbers (the run-gates harness) stays firm; the *number-vs-threshold pass/fail* does not.

## 1. Executive Summary

Roughly 60% of the audit's findings are bookkeeping or arithmetic masquerading as analysis and should be fully mechanized; ~40% retain a creative/semantic core that must stay agentic. The research confirms the direction and sharpens it on one axis: **determinism is not the goal — verifier *validity* is.** A deterministic number can still be the wrong oracle (Theme J), and a deterministic formula over agent-asserted inputs still launders subjectivity (Theme E garbage-in caveat).

The highest-leverage, best-supported moves — all backed by STRONG research and concrete internal examples — are:

1. **Derive every verdict from an oracle, never self-report** (audit P1/P2/P13/P14; research Theme A/B STRONG). Recompute GO/NO-GO, ship-BLOCK, COMPLETED, RED/GREEN from the findings array and gate exit codes; reject self-reported mismatches. Critically, treat a gate that *did not run* as NO-GO, not as a pass (research Theme B: distinguish passed / failed / not-reported).
2. **Manufacture oracles where no reference answer exists** (research Theme C STRONG; Csmith/YARPGen/metamorphic) — strengthens audit's reproduction/regression proposals beyond mere characterization.
3. **Lint artifact structure and traceability** (audit P4/P5/P8; research Theme G MODERATE, Theme K MODERATE-STRONG, Mäder & Egyed: +21% faster, +60% more correct with traceability) — necessary-not-sufficient; structure-green ≠ correct.
4. **Remove flakiness before gating on any test** (research Theme H STRONG prerequisite; Luo FSE'14) — a prerequisite the audit under-weights.
5. **Make severity/confidence a rubric over evidence class** (audit P6; research Theme E MODERATE) — reproducible bands, but the rubric must score evidence the agent *recorded*, never inputs the agent *chose* to hit a band.
6. **Code-metric thresholds become advisory, not gates** (audit P3/P7/P19 DOWNGRADED; research Theme J WEAK-CONTESTED; Shepperd, Landman, Kapser-Godfrey, McCabe).

## 3. Themed Narrative

### 3.1 Verdicts from an oracle (FIRM — STRONG) — audit Theme A, research A/B
The audit's single most dangerous pattern is a status (`GO`, `VALIDATED`, `COMPLETED`, `mergeable`, `RED/GREEN`) written by the agent with nothing recomputing it — an agent can emit `GO` over an OPEN HIGH finding (`roster-review.md` Step 6). The research makes this the strongest of all recommendations: Huang et al. (ICLR'24) and Stechly/Kambhampati (ICML'24) show intrinsic self-correction does not improve and often degrades reasoning, while a **sound external verifier** gives large gains — and the critique *content does not matter*, only the sound pass/fail signal. So the verdict must be a pure function over the findings array and gate exit codes (C1/C4/C5), rendered from captured outputs (C11), with any self-reported mismatch rejected. One sharp addition from research Theme B the audit under-states: a gate that *did not run* (e.g. a check not wired to the merge event) must be treated as **NO-GO, not pass** (C2) — three states, not two.

### 3.2 Manufactured oracles & the limits of generated tests (FIRM/KEEP — STRONG) — research C/D
The research adds a capability the audit only gestures at: where no reference answer exists, you can still build a *sound* oracle from a stated invariant — metamorphic relations, PBT, differential comparison (C13). Csmith (PLDI'11) found >325 compiler bugs purely by differential testing; this is the strongest positive case for replacing judgment. The flip side (Theme D, STRONG): an automatically *generated* test suite asserts "behavior unchanged," not "behavior correct" — Shamshiri (ASE'15) found a single generated suite detects only 19.9% of 357 Defects4J faults, and on buggy code it pins the bug as expected. So C14 keeps the *correctness* call agentic and uses generated suites only as regression characterization. This directly tempers the audit's "tests pass → COMPLETED" framing.

### 3.3 Flakiness first (FIRM PREREQUISITE — STRONG) — research H
The audit's test-gated proposals (P7/P14/P27/P38) all silently assume tests are deterministic. The research flags this as a prerequisite (Theme H, Luo FSE'14: Async-Wait ~45%, Concurrency ~20%, Test-Order ~12% of flaky failures): a test is a deterministic verifier *only if the harness is deterministic*; flakiness converts a sound oracle into noise. C12 therefore precedes every test-gating proposal: quarantine, don't promote, pin seeds.

### 3.4 Structure & traceability linting (FIRM, structure-only — MODERATE / MODERATE-STRONG) — audit E, research G/K
The audit proposes a linter family — `check-brief.sh`, the spec linter, the citation validator (C6/C7/C8/C17/C32). Research Theme K gives traceability strong empirical backing (Mäder & Egyed EMSE'15: +21% faster, +60% more correct solutions; Rempel & Mäder TSE'17: more complete traceability → lower defect rates). But Theme G is explicit that these are **necessary, not sufficient**: schema-valid ≠ semantically true. So every structure linter gates the *human* review, it does not replace it — a green linter proves the graph is well-formed, never that the content is right.

### 3.5 Severity/confidence rubric — and its garbage-in limit (MODERATE) — audit C, research E
The audit's P6 makes confidence an enum keyed to evidence class and severity a lookup over (category × magnitude × critical-path glob). Research Theme E supports the *form* (CVSS v4's MacroVector yields deterministic bands; Leng *Taming Overconfidence* shows RLHF concentrates verbalized confidence at 80–100% with ECE≥0.30, so an executable check beats a self-reported probability — though that comparison is a sound *inference*, not a measured head-to-head, and is flagged as such). The decisive caveat: **a deterministic formula over agent-asserted inputs is still subjective.** Each metric input needs its own evidence, and the harness must not let an agent pick metric values to hit a desired band. That is why C9 makes the *label-consistency* a firm gate (the label must equal the lookup over recorded evidence) but leaves *severity assignment itself* — is this actually a security bug? — agentic, with overrides that can only raise.

### 3.6 The central reframing: code-metric thresholds are advisory, not gates (DOWNGRADED — WEAK-CONTESTED) — audit B/P3/P7/P19, research J
This is the most important reframing in the document. The audit (Theme B, P3/P7/P19) wants to wire the declared thresholds — `max_function_lines: 50`, `max_file_lines: 500`, `max_duplication_threshold: 0.15` — to real tooling (lizard, jscpd, PMD-CPD) and emit a finding when measured value breaches the tunable. The *measurement* half is sound and stays (C11/C25 capture exact integers reproducibly). But the research (Theme J, WEAK-CONTESTED) shows treating a fixed threshold as a **correctness/defect oracle is folklore**:
- Shepperd (1988): cyclomatic complexity is a proxy for, and often outperformed by, LOC; Landman/Serebrenik/Vinju (JSEP'16, 17.6M Java methods) find only *moderate* CC↔LOC correlation — CC's incremental value over SLOC is small and inconsistent.
- McCabe (1976) himself called the threshold 10 "reasonable, not magical"; **no canonical study fits 10/15/20 as a defect-optimal cutpoint** — tools adopt them by convention.
- Kapser & Godfrey (WCRE'06, *"Cloning Considered Harmful" Considered Harmful*) and Rahman et al. (MSR'10): clones are *not* consistently more defect-prone, sometimes less.

So C25/C26/C37 are **downgraded from firm pass/fail gates to advisory, locally-calibrated maintainability signals.** They surface a number for human triage; they do not block a merge or a COMPLETED. Hard-gating on these numbers dresses folklore as determinism and, worse, is itself Goodhart-prone — you "pass" a complexity gate by splitting one complex function into several tightly-coupled small ones (Theme I / Theme J both flag this). The audit's own Keep-Agentic section already says a 600-line generated file can be fine while a 600-line god-object is not; the research turns that intuition into a citation-backed policy.

### 3.7 Goodhart / disjointness (FIRM anti-gaming — STRONG) — research I
A capability the audit treats only implicitly. Any proxy used as a target gets gamed — *including a deterministic one*. EvilGenie reports hacking rates of 36–75% (hard-code tests, edit the harness, special-case visible tests); SpecBench shows the visible−held-out gap grows ~28pp per 10× LOC and that **adding more visible tests sometimes widened the gap by 25pp**. The defense (C16) is structural: the suite the agent optimizes must be **disjoint** from the oracle that decides pass/fail, with tamper detection (test-file-edit detection, mutation testing of the tests, differential checks). This is why determinism alone is insufficient — only oracle *validity + disjointness* protects against gaming.

### 3.8 Detection vs triage (FIRM detection, KEEP triage — MODERATE-STRONG) — audit D, research F
Detection tasks (does this term appear? does this antipattern occur?) become rule engines: Vale/proselint for weasel words (C18), semgrep/ast-grep/CodeQL for antipatterns (C19/C40). The agent then triages the exhaustive hit list — turning "report every finding" from an exhortation into a reproducible scan. Two research caveats bound this: OSS single-file matchers (Semgrep CE) miss cross-file flows, so an **absence result is not proof of absence** (C19 keeps absence claims agentic); and vendor accuracy numbers are CE-not-Pro, directional only.

## 4. Keep-Agentic — Judgment That Must Not Be Mechanized

The over-correction hazard. Mechanizing these would manufacture false confidence or reward vacuous artifacts. The audit's boundaries (preserved) plus those the research surfaces (added):

**From the audit:**
- **Adversarial review reasoning** (`roster-review` 3b) — breaking a change, spotting Goodhart/vacuous tests, blast-radius. Mechanize only *proof the pass ran* (C27), never its quality.
- **Severity/priority *assignment* per finding** (review, audit, architect, qa, spec) — the rubric removes drift; the call (is this a real security bug? P0 vs P1?) stays human, override may only raise (C9).
- **DIVERGE detection** (spec-compliance, audit) — a tag proves a test *exists*, never that behavior *matches* the spec; DIVERGE must cite the specific behavioral difference (C20).
- **Root-cause synthesis** (`investigate`) — connecting evidence into a causal story and choosing which hypothesis to test first; only the post-test confidence label is evidence-derived (C29).
- **Natural-language contradiction detection** (`ambiguity-auditor` 4, `kb-agent`) — prose contradiction is not a diff; only the structured-field subset (numbers, owners, enums, bounds) is mechanizable (C41).
- **"What vs how" mode selection** (`roster-run`) — deterministic layer only *catches contradictions after the fact* (C35), never pre-decides the mode.
- **Adversarial-interview answer quality** (`roster-init` Mode A) — judging substantive vagueness vs merely lacking a number; only count and metric-token presence are safe.
- **"One logical change" commit grouping** (`ship`) — only the lintable surface (format, length, casing) is mechanical (C22).
- **Cross-module coupling / architecture-fit** (`architect`) — not reducible to a line count.
- **Free-text friction "same workaround" merge** (`skill-health`) — LLM merge on top of deterministic field grouping (C24).
- **Whether a clone is *worth* refactoring / a duplicate is intentional** — detection is mechanical, the refactor decision is judgment; security/race/behavior-change rows never auto-fix (C26).

**Added by the research:**
- **Deterministic metric ≠ valid metric** (Theme J, WEAK-CONTESTED). A reproducible CC/length/duplication number is *not* a correctness oracle. The decision of whether a breach matters is judgment — the number is advisory input (C25/C26).
- **A severity *formula over agent-asserted inputs* is still subjective** (Theme E garbage-in caveat). CVSS-style determinism does not launder a metric value the agent chose to hit a band. The evidence behind each input stays a judgment subject to anti-sycophancy.
- **Absence-of-finding from a single-file matcher is not proof of absence** (Theme F). "No semgrep hit" on a cross-file flow is not a clean bill — the absence claim stays agentic (C19).
- **Correctness from generated tests** (Theme D). A green generated suite proves regression-stability, not correctness — the correctness call stays human (C14).
- **Mechanism is "validity," not "determinism."** Across every theme, the load-bearing property is verifier *soundness/disjointness*, not that the output is the same each run. A flaky-but-sound test must be fixed (C12); a deterministic-but-invalid threshold must be demoted (C25).

**The recurring guardrail:** a schema-green or lint-green artifact must never be read as "correct." The linter proves the structure is well-formed (Theme G, necessary-not-sufficient); the human/substance gate stays.
